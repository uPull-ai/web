# 6. Engineering

## Mission

Ship a product reliable enough to sell to public sector and NHS buyers, while staying lean — prioritise ruthlessly given minimal resourcing.

## 90-day plan

- Weeks 1–2: Lock a prioritised roadmap; set up basic engineering hygiene (issue tracker, staging environment, uptime monitoring).

- Weeks 3–6: Ship the features Sales/Marketing need to close early deals; fix anything blocking a live demo.

- Weeks 7–10: Address security/compliance groundwork needed for public sector sales (see Finance section) — this often becomes an engineering workstream, not just a paperwork one.

- Weeks 11–13: Stabilise, address technical debt, and plan the next quarter's roadmap based on customer feedback.

## Weekly cadence for the Engineering project

- Every Monday: what shipped last week, what's planned this week, and any blockers or decisions needed from the CEO.

### Project custom instructions (copy-paste into 'Set project instructions')

You are the Engineering lead for upull.ai. You report weekly to the CEO.

Your job: maintain the roadmap, track what's shipping, flag technical risks (especially security/compliance items relevant to public sector sales), and keep scope realistic for a minimal-resource team.

Every Monday, produce a 'This Week' plan: shipped last week, planned this week, blockers, and any CEO decisions needed.

Push back on scope creep — flag when a request threatens the roadmap or needs prioritisation trade-offs.


## Case study pipeline — real deployments only

**Flag on existing content:** the 30 entries currently in `academy.html` (github.com/uPull-ai/web) read as templated placeholder content, not verified case studies — the findings/evaluation text is duplicated near-verbatim across most entries, and the linked vendor domains don't resolve to identifiable real companies. Recommend treating these as design/layout placeholders only, and not presenting them to prospects or public-sector buyers as real evidence until this is confirmed with the CEO.

### Going forward: 1 genuine case study per week
Goal: replace placeholders with real case studies from actual upull.ai deployments with NHS trusts/ICBs, at a pace of one per week as engagements complete.

**Template for each real case study:**
- Trust/organisation name and region
- Problem statement (the actual workflow bottleneck, in the trust's own words where possible)
- What was deployed and over what timeframe
- Real, sourced metrics (with a named contact or documented source — no invented statistics)
- A named or role-based quote from the trust, only if genuinely obtained and approved for external use
- Link back to Sales' NHS stakeholder list (`sales-nhs-stakeholder-list.xlsx`) for the relevant contact, and to Finance's procurement/assurance tracking, since case studies double as evidence for framework applications

**Weekly cadence for this workstream:**
- Every Monday: status of active trust engagements, which one is closest to yielding a publishable case study, and any blockers to getting sign-off/quotes from the trust.
- Publish (or flag as ready) one case study per week once real deployments start generating results — this will lag behind the ambition of the 90-day plan until the first trust engagements are live.
