# 4. Finance & Accounting

## Mission

Keep the company financially sound and compliant, and build the specific credibility needed to sell into UK public sector and NHS buyers — a market with its own procurement, assurance, and payment norms.

## Core responsibilities

- Bookkeeping, cash flow tracking, and basic management accounts (even pre-revenue).

- UK company compliance: Companies House filings, Corporation Tax, VAT registration threshold monitoring, payroll if/when you hire.

- Pricing and unit economics — make sure what Sales sells is priced sustainably.

## UK public sector & NHS-specific guidance

- Framework routes: public sector buyers largely procure via approved frameworks (e.g. G-Cloud on the Crown Commercial Service Digital Marketplace, NHS's Spark DPS, or the NHS Digital Care Services Framework). Getting listed on the relevant framework is usually the fastest legitimate route to being purchasable at all, rather than relying on direct outreach.

- DPS vs framework: Dynamic Purchasing Systems (like Spark) allow new suppliers to join at any time, unlike closed frameworks with fixed application windows — worth prioritising for a young startup.

- Assurance requirements: expect to need Cyber Essentials (and often Cyber Essentials Plus) certification, a Data Protection Impact Assessment, and — for anything touching patient data — DSPT (Data Security and Protection Toolkit) compliance and possibly DTAC (Digital Technology Assessment Criteria).

- Payment terms: public sector bodies are contractually required to pay undisputed invoices within 30 days; NHS trusts can be slower in practice, so plan cash flow conservatively (60–90 day realistic buffer) in early deals.

- Pricing: public sector rate cards need to be transparent and defensible — avoid variable/opaque pricing that's hard to justify in a procurement evaluation.

This is a starting checklist, not legal or financial advice — validate framework requirements and current thresholds directly with Crown Commercial Service and NHS Digital before committing time to an application, since these details change.
